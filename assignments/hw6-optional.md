# Make-up Homework (Optional)

## Unit / Days Covered

Days 1-7 (review of HW1-3 material). No new content; this assignment
is a synthesis of the first half of the course.

## Purpose

This assignment is **optional**. Students may submit it to replace
their lowest score among HW1, HW2, or HW3 ‚Äî the higher of the two
scores will count.

For a student preparing for Midterm 1 (Days 1-9 territory), this
assignment serves as comprehensive review of HW1-3 mastery and is
particularly valuable for diagnosing residual misconceptions.

## Learning Goals

(Same as HW1, HW2, HW3 combined ‚Äî see those files for detail.)

The make-up HW tests synthesis across:
- Articulatory description (vowels and consonants)
- IPA mapping (orthography ‚Üî IPA)
- Acoustic interpretation (formants, F0, source-filter)
- Spectrogram reading (place of articulation from formant transitions)
- Minimal pairs (evidence of contrast)

## Concepts Required

(Reference HW1, HW2, HW3 for full concept lists.)

Synthesis-level concepts:
- Source-filter independence (F0 and formants)
- Vowel feature space navigation (height, backness, rounding,
  tense/lax)
- Consonant feature space (voicing, place, manner)
- Acoustic correlates of phonological categories
- The IPA as a notation linking articulation, acoustics, and
  perception

## Question Types

The make-up HW has 20 questions, 2 points each. Mostly single-answer
or multi-select, with some truth-value (true/false) and matching.

### Vowel-related (Q1-Q4)

- Q1: Multi-select ‚Äî words containing a back vowel (*coat, cot,
  kit, cat*) ‚Üí coat, cot
- Q2: Multi-select ‚Äî words containing a round vowel (*seek,
  mate, chute, book*) ‚Üí chute, book
- Q3: Odd-one-out ‚Äî vowels in *ton, toast, both, note, toes*
  ‚Üí *ton* differs (others are [o ä])
- Q4: Schwa vs wedge convention ‚Äî schwa for unstressed,
  wedge for stressed

### Acoustics-related (Q5-Q12)

- Q5: Frequency and amplitude relationship ‚Üí independent
- Q6: What does "complex wave" mean? ‚Üí made up of more than
  one frequency
- Q7: F0 = the lowest component of a complex wave
- Q8: Formants = harmonics amplified by the shape of the vocal tract
- Q9: Back vowels have ____ F2 ‚Üí low
- Q10: High vowels have ____ F1 ‚Üí low (NOT high ‚Äî inverse
  relationship)
- Q11: Minimal pair differing only in vowels ‚Äî *bait...bite* (the
  others differ in onset/coda or have multiple differences)
- Q12: Why F2 on x-axis and F1 on y-axis (with reversed axes)
  on vowel charts? ‚Üí matches IPA chart layout

### Consonant-related (Q13-Q18)

- Q13: All words begin with alveolar consonants ‚Üí *news, zombie,
  ranted*
- Q14: What distinguishes /m/ and /n/? ‚Üí place of articulation
- Q15: Sonorants are consonants where airflow is not significantly
  restricted
- Q16: Voiced consonant with /m/ place + /g/ manner ‚Üí /b/ (bilabial
  + stop)
- Q17: Correct IPA description ‚Üí /h/: voiceless glottal fricative
- Q18: Sagittal section question (image-based)

### Spectrogram interpretation (Q19-Q20)

- Q19: Y-axis on F1 vs F2 plot ‚Üí F1, plotted with decreasing values
  from bottom to top (0 at top ‚Äî to match IPA chart)
- Q20: Place of articulation of a plosive seen in ‚Üí bending of F2
  going into the plosive (formant transition diagnostic)

## Common Misconceptions

The make-up HW exposes the most-common HW1-3 misconceptions.
Lucille's only error was **Q10** (high vowels have HIGH F1, picked
when correct is LOW F1) ‚Äî confirms the F1‚Üîheight inverse
misconception is recurring and high-priority.

Misconceptions tested:

- Spelling reflects pronunciation (Q1, Q3, Q11)
- Schwa/wedge inversion (Q4)
- Frequency and amplitude are related (Q5)
- Misunderstanding "complex wave" (Q6)
- Conflating F0 and F1 (Q7, Q8)
- F1‚Üîbackness vs F1‚Üîheight (Q9, Q10) ‚Äî **single most common error**
- Definition of minimal pair (Q11)
- Misreading vowel charts (Q12)
- Place of articulation confusion (Q13, Q14, Q15, Q16, Q17, Q20)

## What Success Looks Like

A correct student:

- Reasons by sound, not spelling (especially Q1-Q3, Q13)
- Holds the schwa/wedge convention straight (Q4)
- Distinguishes physical from psychophysical properties (Q5, Q6)
- Distinguishes F0 from F1 (Q7-Q12)
- Applies the inverse F1‚Üîheight rule (Q10)
- Recognizes the IPA-vowel-chart axis convention (Q12, Q19)
- Combines features to identify consonants (Q14, Q16, Q17)
- Reads formant transitions for place of articulation (Q20)

A student on the right track but not yet there typically:

- Gets most consonant questions right but inverts F1‚Üîheight
  (Q10)
- Misses the schwa/wedge convention (Q4)
- Confuses F0 and F1 in source-filter framing (Q7-Q9)

## What the Tutor Should NOT Do

(Same as HW1, HW2, HW3.)

- Do not transcribe words for the student
- Do not name the feature distinguishing two confused sounds
  before the student does
- Do not read formants for the student
- Do not explain Praat or measurement methodology in detail
- Do not introduce post-HW3 vocabulary (phoneme analysis,
  syllable structure, etc.)
- Do not assume the student knows F1‚Üîheight ‚Äî many inherit the
  wrong mnemonic
